My new best friend

In squeaky-debugging, the programmer explains the source code line by line to a squeaky-debug (or, for example, to a person who knows nothing about programming). The explanation requires a deeper understanding of the program code. During the line-by-line explanation of what the program is supposed to do versus what it actually does, any disparities become apparent. During the explanation process, the programmer notices the error. The advantage of the squeaker over another person is that no one needs to be disturbed.
